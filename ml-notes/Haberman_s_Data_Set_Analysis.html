Data Set : Haberman's survival data¶

Description:(attributes):

   Age of patient at time of operation (numerical)

   Patient's year of operation (year - 1900, numerical)

   Number of positive axillary nodes detected (numerical)

   Survival status (class attribute):

       1 = the patient survived 5 years or longer

       2 = the patient died within 5 year

Objective:

   To analyze the data and tell what category of patients have survived more or less
   than 5 years,
   key attributes/parameters we are gonna use are Age and Axillary Nodes as Year of
   operation tells us nothing required for our objective.

Conclusion:¶

Age doesn't seems to do much with chances of survival.
It all depends on Number of positive axillary nodes(From Wikipedia,    
positive axillary lymph node is a lymph node in the area of the armpit (axilla) to which cancer 
has spread).

patients with 0 axillary nodes have survived more than 5 years,
38% of the times and 6% of times hasn't(most likely because of age factor or
previous health issues).

patients with axillary nodes between 1 and 3 have survived more than 5 years,
20% of  the times and 7% of the times didn't.

I've taken consideratoins till 3rd quartile only here and have not given much
importance to extremities in our data as those few are exceptionally very lucky
patients who've survived against the odds.
